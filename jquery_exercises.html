Full Moon Will Turn An Eerie Red During The Total Lunar Eclipse

By Taylor Nicioli and Ashley Strickland, CNN
Updated 3:06 PM EST, Mon November 7, 2022

(CNN) — Set to turn a coppery shade of red in the sky this Tuesday, November 8, the full moon will kick off Election Day with an early morning event of its own — a total lunar eclipse. The second one of the year, the eclipse will begin at 3:02 a.m. ET, with the moon initially dimming for the first hour, and end at 8:50 a.m. ET. At totality, the stage at which the entire moon will be in Earth’s shadow, the moon will turn a dark reddish hue, which is why a total eclipse is also called a blood moon. Sky gazers will be able to see the striking effect beginning at 5:17 a.m. ET, according to NASA.
